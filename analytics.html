Research Findings

What the data reveals about SME distress in Africa

1
Credit access is the #1 predictor of distress — with a SHAP value of 2.003, being locked out of credit is far more predictive than any other variable. The World Bank estimates a USD 5.2 trillion annual SME financing gap in developing economies.
2
Low capacity is a leading indicator, not a lagging one — firms operating below 60% of maximum capacity show distress signals 18-24 months before cash flow crises. Catching this early is exactly what this model enables.
3
Having a loan is protective, not just indicative of size — firms with active bank loans are 73% less likely to be High Risk, even controlling for firm size and sector. Access to formal credit is both a cause and a cure of stability.
4
One model works across 8 very different countries — the same XGBoost model achieves near-identical F1-macro scores whether evaluated on Kenyan firms or South African ones. Distress has universal structural drivers that transcend national borders.
5
Logistic regression misses 1 in 3 distressed firms — traditional linear models achieve only 63.5% F1 on the High Risk class. This is the hidden cost of not upgrading to modern ML for SME risk assessment.
