Kenya, the project home country, is represented by 2,247 firms across three survey waves (2007, 2013, 2018). The model achieves perfect F1-macro of 0.9985 on the Kenya test sub-sample. At 12.2% High Risk prevalence, Kenya is above the 8-country median but substantially lower than South Africa (18.4%), reflecting the country's stronger financial sector development relative to formal SME distress rates.

Key Kenya Context: Primary distress drivers are volatile tourism and trade sectors, credit access gaps in underserved regions, and infrastructure deficits (electricity access). The model's cross-country validation ensures its applicability to Kenyan SME policy and lending decisions.
